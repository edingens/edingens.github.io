Why This Matters

Packaging waste has become one of the most pressing environmental issues. Every minute, one million plastic bottles are bought globally, but only 9% are recycled.

The environmental impact spans beyond what the eye sees, contributing to pollution, climate change, and resource depletion. Together, we can rethink packaging habits and move toward sustainable alternatives.

The Problem with Single-Use Packaging

Environmental Impact

Single-use plastics contribute to pollution, harm wildlife, and accelerate climate change. The cost of production is staggering, as are the consequences of improper disposal.

Waste Statistics

Plastic waste has increased exponentially over time. About 79% of plastic waste ends up in landfills or the environment, polluting oceans and ecosystems.

Microplastics

Microplastics contaminate water, food, and even our bodies. This creates a cycle of health risks, impacting both humans and animals.
